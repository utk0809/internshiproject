Email Security Guide

Master the art of deconstructing email headers to expose cyber threats and verify routing paths.

◆ SPOOFING AWARENESS

Cyber attackers use various techniques to deceive recipients. Here is how we catch them:

  • Header Mismatch: Legitimate emails usually have matching From and Return-Path domains. Mismatches are a major red flag.
  • Typosquatting: Attackers use domains like paypαl.com instead of paypal.com. Always check for subtle character swaps.
  • Display Name Deception: Always look past the "Sender Name" to the actual email address in brackets.
  • Protocol Failures: If SPF or DKIM checks fail, the email is likely forged.

◆ SMTP SECURITY PROTOCOLS

To secure the plain SMTP protocol, these technologies were added to verify identity:

  • SPF (Sender Policy Framework): Lists authorized IP addresses for a domain.
  • DKIM (DomainKeys Identified Mail): Adds a digital signature to the email content.
  • DMARC: Instructs the receiver how to handle emails that fail SPF or DKIM.

◆ EMAIL ROUTING TRANSIT

Emails travel through Mail Transfer Agents (MTA). Each hop adds a timestamped Received header with the handling server's identity and IP.

Traces reveal the geographic and network origin, helping security analysts bypass fake labels.
